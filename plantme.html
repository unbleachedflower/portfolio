Plant Me
Website Redesign

Overview

Have you ever wanted to decorate your own place with plants but didn’t know where to start? You turned to Google but only find yourself frustrated with the information overload. That’s why we created PlantMe - a monthly plant subscription service that helps people build their home garden. We provide “just enough” guidance to make sure your plants can stay healthy and alive.

01  Problem

Users also run into the problem of plant caretaking. Often, they are frustrated with either lack of or overload information.

02  Competitive Analysis

No guide on helping customers identify the plant suitable to their life styles.

No clear instruction on how to take care of house plants.

03  User Interview

We sat down with 8 interviewees and ask them questions about what are they struggling with in terms of home planting? What are they looking for in relating services.

04  Solution

“Simply answering 10 quick questions, users will be matched with plants that are suitable to raise in accordance to their life styles.”

05  User Roadmap

Initial roadmap was drawn to ensure the streamlined user experience. This also helps determine the site information structure and backend data schema.

06  Wireframe

Wireframes was then discussed, drawn, and agreed upon for main pages such as the homepage, PlantWiki page, user profile page and e-commerce purchase and checkout process pages.

07  Prototype

“ We applied logic between the pages according to our user roadmap, added missing pages, deleted redundant content. "

08  Style Tile

We selected a breathing, vivid color palette. We choose the “Noteworthy” as our header font style to give the website more of an organic feel. and the “Geneva” as our content font style to make the content pages modern and clear.
