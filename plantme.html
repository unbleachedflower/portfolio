Plant Me
Website Redesign

Overview

Have you ever wanted to decorate your place with plants but did not know where to start? You turned to Google but only found yourself frustrated with the information overload.

That is why we created PlantMe - a monthly plant subscription service that helps people build their home garden. We provide “just enough” information for you to identify plants that suit your lifestyle and some guidance to make sure they can stay healthy and alive.

01  Problem

1 | Users cannot decide on what plant to grow that would fit their lifestyle.

2 | Users also run into the problem of plant caretaking. Often, they are given either too little or too much information.

02  Competitive Analysis

Most of the competitors' sites we encountered have two significant problems:

1 | There is little to none guidance on helping customers to identify a plant that fits their lifestyle.

2 | Instructions on how to take care of house plants are either limited or unclear.

03  User Interview

We interviewed eight people to find out what their struggles were in terms of plant caretaking. At the same time, we asked if they could suggest services that could solve their problems.

04  Solution

“By answering ten simple questions, users will be matched with plants that fit their lifestyles.”

05  User Roadmap

We identified the roadmap that would provide an optimized user experience. Afterwards, we determined the site information structure, along with the data schema.

06  Wireframes

Wireframes were then discussed, drawn, and agreed upon for main pages such as the homepage, PlantWiki page, user profile page, and e-commerce pages.

07  Prototype

“In the next step, we applied logics between the pages. We also added missing pages and deleted repeated content. "

08  Design Style Tile

The colors we picked for the style tile were all vivid and vibrant. We hope that this color palette would make users feel refreshed and engaged when they visit the site. Notably, the font “Noteworthy” was selected as the first font and “Geneva” as the secondary font. Together, they delivered a design solution that represents both modern and original qualities.
